Top tracks

The bands debut album is the best selling debut album in British history, the album features some of the groups most famous songs & received critical acclaim with songs depiciting the experiences of youth for the band, resonating with young people in the north of England especially, with many references to their hometown of Sheffield. The album would eventually sell over 2 million copies in the UK (7x Platinum), the album's most recognisable tracks are:

  • I Bet You Look Good On The Dancefloor
  • Mardy Bum
  • When The Sun Goes Down
  • A Certain Romance
  • From The Ritz To The Rubble
  • The View From The Afternoon
  • Fake Tales Of San Fransisco

While "The View From The Afternoon" was not released as a single it quickly became a fan favourite & a staple for live performances. As with many of the bands early tracks the vocals still hold a significant northern accent, Witty lyricism & a strong heavy hitting drum style; the video was also filmed at a local sheffield landmark, the parkhill estate, at the time being a symbol of decay in the area.
